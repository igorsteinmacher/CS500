# HW6 - Intro to programming languages

Your company wants to choose the programming language they will use in their next projects. The management will hire a totally new team to work on these project, so there is no worry to keep anything that the company already uses. After a meeting with the management, you are asked to study which would be the most appropriate programming languages, and provide an executive summary with your first and second choices. You need to consider the following requirements and constraints brought up by that the management:

- The company really cares about green IT, so they want to reduce the carbon footprint that compiling/running the program would have
- The projects will deal with Data Analytics, dealing with big data chunks
- There must be a good amount of developers in the market, since this is a big and new project
- The company does not want to spend money with tools (IDEs, compilers/interpreters, linters)
- All employees use a standard computer with the same architecture and Operating System
- It should be easy enough for new junior developers to find free training materials

Your deliverable should be easy to understand, backed by evidence, and clearly touch all the points listed above. Although a too-long summary should be avoided, you are expected that this is comprehensive enough, and the length should be between 3-5 pages (Arial 12, single space).

You may have to research about some topics. Make sure that you correctly reference the sources where you get your information from.

You can rely on sources, using with moderation. Plagiarism will be punished as usual (creating an essay using massive amount of third part text is plagiarism). You can read, understand, and create YOUR narrative.

You will be evaluted in terms of:

- Your ability to argue about the topic
- The appropriateness of your choice and each of the requirements provided
- Connection with the design constraints and language evaluation criteria
- Trustability of your arguments (sources, references, comparisons)
- Textual coherence and use of appropriate language
